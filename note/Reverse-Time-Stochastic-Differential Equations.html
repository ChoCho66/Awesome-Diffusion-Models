Reverse Time Stochastic Differential Equations

  • Consider a process (X_t)_{t\in [0,1]} which satisfies the SDE \begin{aligned} \mathrm{d}X_t = \mu(X_t,t)\mathrm{d}t + \sigma(X_t,t) \mathrm{d}B_t, \quad t\in [0,1]. \end{aligned} Let \overline{X}_{\tau}:=X_{1-\tau},\tau\in [0,1] be the reverse process of (X_t)_{t\in [0,1]}. Then (\overline{X}_\tau)_{\tau \in [0,1]} satisfies the SDE, for t=1-\tau, \begin{aligned} \mathrm{d}\overline{X}_\tau = \underline{\biggl( -\mu(\overline{X}_\tau) + \frac{1}{p(\overline{X}_\tau,t)} \partial_x \Bigl( \sigma(x,t)^2 p(x,t) \Bigr) \bigg\vert_{x=\overline{X}_\tau} \biggr)} \mathrm{d}\tau + \underline{\sigma(\overline{X}_\tau,t)} d\overline{B}_\tau, \end{aligned} where p(\cdot,t) is the density of X_t.

  • If we set \begin{aligned} \overline{\sigma}(\tau):=\sigma(1-\tau), \quad \overline{p}(\cdot,\tau):= p(\cdot,1-\tau), \end{aligned} then the SDE above becomes \begin{aligned} \mathrm{d}\overline{X}_\tau = \underline{\biggl( -\mu(\overline{X}_\tau) + \frac{1}{\overline{p}(\overline{X}_\tau,\tau)} \partial_x \Bigl( \overline{\sigma}(x,\tau)^2 \overline{p}(x,\tau) \Bigr) \bigg\vert_{x=\overline{X}_\tau} \biggr)} \mathrm{d}\tau + \underline{\overline{\sigma}(\overline{X}_\tau,\tau)} d\overline{B}_\tau, \quad \tau\in [0,1]. \end{aligned} If \sigma(x,t) is independent of x, i.e. \sigma(x,t)=\sigma(t), then \begin{aligned} \mathrm{d}\overline{X}_\tau = \underline{\biggl( -\mu(\overline{X}_\tau) + \overline{\sigma}(\tau)^2 \partial_x \Bigl( \log \overline{p}(x,\tau) \Bigr) \bigg\vert_{x=\overline{X}_\tau} \biggr)} \mathrm{d}\tau + \underline{\overline{\sigma}(\tau)} d\overline{B}_\tau, \quad \tau\in [0,1]. \end{aligned}

  • Sketch of proof:

    Let p(\cdot,t) be the density of X_t. Since (X_t)_{t\in [0,1]} satisfies the SDE \begin{aligned} \mathrm{d}X_t = \mu(X_t,t)\mathrm{d}t + \sigma(X_t,t) \mathrm{d}B_t, \quad t\in [0,1], \end{aligned} we have the Kolmogorov forward equation (KFE), also called Fokker Planck equation, \begin{aligned} \partial_t p(x,t) = -\partial_x \bigl( \mu(x,t) p(x,t) \bigr) + \frac{1}{2} \partial_x^2 \bigl( \sigma(x,t)^2 p(x,t) \bigr). \end{aligned} Let p(\cdot,s\vert x,t), s\geq t, be the conditional density of X_s given X_t=x. Also, we have the Kolmogorov backward equation (KBF) \begin{aligned} -\partial_t p(y,s\vert x,t) = \mu(x,t) \partial_x p(y,s\vert x,t) + \frac{1}{2} \sigma(x,t)^2 \partial_x^2 p(y,s\vert x,t), \quad t\leq s. \end{aligned}

    \begin{aligned} -\partial_t p(x,t) = \mu(x,t) \partial_x p(x,t) + \frac{1}{2}\sigma(x,t)^2 \partial_x^2 p(x,t), \quad t\leq s. \end{aligned}
